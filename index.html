01. Reduces the risk of heart disease:

Regular blood donation has been associated with a lower risk of cardiovascular diseases. Donating blood helps maintain healthy blood viscosity and reduces the level of iron in the body. High iron levels have been linked to an increased risk of heart disease, so regular blood donation can help reduce that risk.

02. Stimulates the production of new blood cells:

After donating blood, the body works to replenish the lost blood cells. This process stimulates the production of new blood cells in the bone marrow, leading to the regeneration and rejuvenation of the body's blood supply. This can have a positive impact on overall health and well-being.

03. Provides a free health check-up:

Prior to donating blood, donors undergo a thorough screening process that includes blood pressure measurement, hemoglobin level testing, and other health assessments. This provides an opportunity to identify any potential health issues or abnormalities that may require further medical attention. It can serve as a basic health check-up and help donors stay aware of their well-being.

04. Promotes psychological well-being:

Engaging in acts of altruism, such as donating blood, has been linked to improved psychological well-being. Donating blood can evoke a sense of purpose, fulfillment, and satisfaction, knowing that you have made a meaningful contribution to the health and well-being of others. It can also create a sense of community and connectedness with fellow donors and recipients.

05. Improves Blood Circulation:

When you donate blood, your body replenishes the donated blood by producing new blood cells. This process helps to improve blood circulation throughout the body, promoting better oxygen and nutrient delivery to various organs and tissues. Improved blood circulation can have positive effects on overall health and vitality.

Who are Eligible to Donate Blood?

  • 📌 Age between 18 and 60 years
  • 📌 Haemoglobin - not less than 12.5 g/Dl
  • 📌 Pulse - between 50 and 100/minute with no irregularities
  • 📌 Blood Pressure -Systolic 100-180 mm Hg and Diastolic 50 - 100 mm Hg
  • 📌 Temperature - Normal (oral temperature not exceeding 37.50C)
  • 📌 Body weight - not less than 45 Kg
